Situation Brief
Operation Fractured Grid
What participants walk into — 9:30 AM Tuesday
It is 9:30 AM Tuesday. At 7:18 AM, the IT help desk began receiving calls. Employees couldn't log in. By 8:00 AM, the CISO confirmed ransomware is running across Ramsey County's systems. Summit, Aspen, Outlook, property tax systems, Environmental Health, Vital Records, LaserFiche, and county phones are offline or compromised. A ransom note demands $2.3 million.

You are now in this room together. This exercise does not attempt to solve the ransomware. It focuses on three things that cannot wait days — they need a decision and a plan in the next few hours.
✕
No County Computers
All workstations and laptops are inaccessible or untrustworthy. Do not use them.
✕
No County Network
No shared drives, no intranet, no VPN. The county network is fully compromised.
✕
No County Email
Microsoft 365 / Outlook is unavailable. No internal or external county email.
✕
No County Phones
Desk phones, Jabber, and the internal phone system are all down.
⚠
County Cells — Caution
County-issued cell phones may be compromised. Treat with caution.
✓
What You Have
Personal cell phones, personal email, paper, and each other in this room.
Three Problems That Cannot Wait
  • Payroll runs in 48 hours. The system that processes it is encrypted. 2,400 employees. No documented manual backup.
  • Residents have no way to reach us. Ramsey Info Line down. Website unreachable. County phones dead. FAS clients, Housing clients, Public Health — all cut off.
  • Sheriff's RMS is inaccessible. Deputies are on paper. A 1 PM court case, an incomplete evidence chain, and a warrant in unknown status.
What This Exercise Produces
  • Named decisions on all three problems — made in this room, today
  • Identified gaps — things we don't have, don't know, or can't do
  • Named owners with personal contact numbers for each action
  • A starting list for the After-Action Report
  • A shared understanding across all 46 leaders before a real event occurs

How Tables Work
  • 5 tables of ~9 people — all work every scenario simultaneously
  • Table Anchor — one senior leader keeps discussion moving and drives toward a decision
  • Primary Actors — 2–4 named people who speak first in each scenario
  • Lens Holders — everyone else holds a colored card with 3 standing questions they raise in every scenario
  • Table Scribe — one person captures decisions and gaps throughout
Ground Rules
  • Play your actual role — use your real authority and real resources
  • No fault, no blame — gaps found here protect the county from real harm
  • Minimize distractions — personal devices only for exercise participation
  • FREEZE means pause — facilitator controls timing and pivots
  • Work from available information — make decisions without perfect information, as in a real event

⚠ Inject — Read Aloud to All Tables
The payroll processing system sits on one of the encrypted servers. Payroll runs in exactly 48 hours. If it doesn't run, all county employees — deputies, social workers, public health nurses, parks staff, public works crews, library staff, everyone in this room — will not receive their paycheck on time. Union contracts require on-time payment. Late payroll triggers financial penalties. There is no documented manual payroll backup procedure. The payroll vendor's contact information is in a system that is currently encrypted.

Your table has 25 minutes. What do you do?

What is the legal exposure for one day of late payroll? What emergency authorization mechanisms exist in county code?

Facilitator Guide
Context Brief & Facilitation Notes
Read aloud at the start. Then follow the timing guide.
Read Aloud — Slow Down — Make Eye Contact — Pause Between Paragraphs

It is Tuesday morning. At 7:18 AM, the IT help desk began receiving calls. Employees couldn't log in. By 8:00 AM, the CISO confirmed ransomware is running across Ramsey County's systems. Summit, Aspen, Outlook, operational systems — property tax, Environmental Health, Vital Records, LaserFiche — and county phones are offline or compromised. A ransom note is demanding $2.3 million.

It is now 9:30 AM. You are in this room together. Your county laptops are untrustworthy. Your county email is gone. Your desk phones and Jabber don't work. Your county cell phones may be compromised. You have your personal cell phone, the people at your table, some paper, and whatever you can reach through personal channels.

We are not here to fix the ransomware. We are here because three things cannot wait days. They need a decision and a plan in the next few hours.

Number one: Payroll runs in 48 hours. The system that runs it is compromised.
Number two: Residents are calling. They can't reach anyone. County phones are down. They don't know if their benefits are safe or if the county is even open.
Number three: The Sheriff's Records Management System — used for incident reports, case files, and evidence tracking — is inaccessible. Deputies are on paper.

Each table has the people who own these problems — and the people whose lens will make the solution better. Let's start.

Timing
Use a visible countdown timer on a screen. When 5 minutes remain in a scenario: "Five minutes — lens holders, get your question in now." When time is up, say FREEZE. Do not extend. Unfinished discussions become named gaps, which is a valid outcome.
Report-Out
Go table by table. Point to the anchor: "One decision your table made — thirty seconds." Then: "One gap your table found — thirty seconds." Do not allow elaboration. After all five tables, County Manager names three actions with owners. Done.
When Discussion Stalls
If a table goes quiet, throw to a lens holder: "Operations lens — what breaks if this goes wrong?" This almost always restarts conversation.
When Someone Debates the Scenario
Say: "That's a valid point and it may be true — for the purposes of this exercise, assume the condition as stated. What do you do?" Move on.
Closing Line
End with: "Every gap we found today is a win — because we found it here, not at 7:18 AM on a Tuesday when it's real. The After-Action Report will be in your hands within 10 days. Thank you."
